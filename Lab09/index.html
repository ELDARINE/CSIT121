Sel Roti

A Traditional Nepali Delight

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Pinch of turmeric powder (optional, for color)
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Sugar, to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine rice flour, all-purpose flour, mashed bananas, yogurt, cardamom powder, cumin seeds, and baking powder. Mix well to form a thick batter. If desired, add a pinch of turmeric powder for a golden color.
  2. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Make sure it's hot but not smoking.
  3. Take a funnel-shaped plastic or metal funnel (Sel Roti mold) and dip it into the hot oil for a minute to heat it.
  4. Pour a ladleful of the batter into the funnel while blocking the bottom hole with your finger. Slowly release your finger to allow the batter to flow into the hot oil in a circular motion, creating a ring shape. Be careful not to overcrowd the pan; you may need to fry them in batches.
  5. Fry the Sel Roti until they turn golden brown on both sides, turning them occasionally with a spatula. It should take about 4-5 minutes per Sel Roti.
  6. Remove the fried Sel Roti and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  7. Serve hot with a sprinkle of sugar on top. Sel Roti is best enjoyed with tea or yogurt.
